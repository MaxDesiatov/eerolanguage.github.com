about eero

Removing the “Slum of Braces” from Objective-C

My wife once told me about the idea behind the design of the Tulip chair and Pedestal table by the Finnish-American architect Eero Saarinen, who is perhaps best known for designing St. Louis’ Gateway Arch. His goal in creating this table and chairs, which rest on round bases, was to clean up the “slum of legs” that had always been present in dining-room furniture. Coincidentally, Saarinen also had taught at the Cranbrook Institute of Science, which is near my hometown of Detroit and which I had visited as a child. Given that and our mutual interest in reduction and simplification, it seemed fitting to name this dialect after him.
